Margin vs Border vs Padding

Borders, margins, width, height, alignment, properties etc CSS can be very overwhemling so let’s start out by appointing an HMTL item to center our discussion around. Below is a very simple HTML item and within that item is the text "Demo Content".

Demo Content

Here's how the padding, border and margin have been applied to 'the box':

Padding

The content of the box is the text “Demo Content”. Padding allows you to set the space around the content.the padding has been illustrated above by the white area between the content and the border.

Border

The border of the HTML item is represented by the brown outline. The border acts as a perimeter around the content and its padding.

Margin

The orange around the border is called the margin. The margins job is to provide space between the HTML object whichs and the surrounding HTML objects. In the example above the margin is ensuring the text above and below are not impeding on the brown border, white padding or the text content "Demo Content".

Summary

Margin, Border, and Padding is all about space relative to each other, it all starts with content then to present that content we add padding to keep the border from making it difficult to read or interpret the content. Next in line we have the border to either highight or protect the content and finally we have the margin to make sure other object and content does not impeed.
